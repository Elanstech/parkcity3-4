I A Quiet Manifesto

Some buildings ask to be admired. Ours simply asks you to stay.

Park City 3 & 4 was built in 1955 with a single intuition: that a home should hold its residents the way a courtyard holds a quiet afternoon. Seven decades later, that intuition has only grown more deliberate — six buildings, fourteen stories each, and a private terrace at every door.

Across our cooperative you'll find sun-washed lobbies, meticulously kept grounds, and a community whose first instinct is to look out for one another. We are unhurried, attentive, and proudly unimpressed by trends.

— The Shareholders & Stewards of Park City 3 & 4

Quietly Answered

A few honest answers.

01Is Park City 3 & 4 a co-op?

Yes. We are a cooperative community of six buildings. Apartments are owned by shareholders rather than rented from a landlord — an arrangement that has shaped the careful, long-term character of the place.

02Who manages the buildings?

Our day-to-day management is handled by AKAM — one of New York's most respected residential management firms — alongside our own on-site Management Office and Maintenance team, which operate Monday through Friday, 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM.

03Does every apartment have a terrace?

Yes. Every apartment across all six buildings features a private terrace with views of the surrounding neighborhood. It's one of the things this property has always been known for.

04How is parking arranged?

Two 24-hour indoor parking garages serve the community, with assigned spaces. Access is handled through the same keyless / fob system used for the buildings themselves.

05Is there storage available?

Private storage is available in each building, professionally managed by Bargold. Shareholders can inquire with the management office for current availability.

06When is the pool open?

Our outdoor swimming pool is seasonal and managed by a dedicated third-party operator. It opens for the warmer months — exact dates are announced each spring by management.

07How do I reach a superintendent in an emergency?

Each building has a live-in superintendent available for emergencies. During business hours, the on-site Management Office can also be reached directly; after hours, the building's emergency line is the right first call.
